Applying in Bloom's Taxonomy

Applying is the third level of Bloom's Taxonomy, which involves using information in a new way or applying it to a new situation.

Examples of applying include:

  • Using a formula to solve a problem
  • Applying a concept to a real-world situation
  • Creating a new product or design based on existing knowledge
  • Using critical thinking skills to solve a complex problem

Applying is important because it allows learners to use their knowledge in practical and meaningful ways, and helps to develop problem-solving and decision-making skills.

Some strategies for promoting applying include:

  • Providing opportunities for learners to apply their knowledge in real-world situations
  • Encouraging learners to think creatively and come up with new solutions to problems
  • Providing opportunities for learners to work on open-ended projects that require them to apply their knowledge in different ways
  • Encouraging learners to reflect on their learning and how they can apply it to their lives and future goals
