Understanding in Bloom's Taxonomy

Understanding is the second level of Bloom's Taxonomy, which involves making sense of or explaining information that has been learned.

Examples of understanding include:

  • Explaining a concept in your own words
  • Summarizing a reading passage
  • Interpreting a chart or graph
  • Translating information from one form to another

Understanding is important because it helps learners to make connections between different pieces of information and apply their knowledge in new contexts.

Some strategies for promoting understanding include:

  • Asking questions to clarify and deepen understanding
  • Using analogies or real-life examples to make abstract concepts more concrete
  • Encouraging learners to identify patterns and relationships between different pieces of information
  • Providing opportunities for learners to apply their understanding in new situations
