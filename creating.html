Creating in Bloom's Taxonomy

Creating is the highest level of Bloom's Taxonomy, which involves using knowledge and skills to produce something new or original.

Examples of creating include:

  • Designing a new product, system, or process
  • Developing a new theory, concept, or hypothesis
  • Writing a novel, play, or screenplay
  • Producing a film, video, or multimedia project

Creating is important because it helps learners to develop innovation, imagination, and entrepreneurship skills, and to apply their knowledge and skills in new and meaningful ways.

Some strategies for promoting creating include:

  • Encouraging learners to generate ideas and to explore new possibilities
  • Providing opportunities for learners to experiment, prototype, and test their ideas
  • Providing access to resources and tools that support creativity and innovation
  • Encouraging learners to collaborate and share ideas with others
